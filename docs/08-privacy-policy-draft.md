# SBBC Privacy Policy Draft

> Draft for planning only. This document is not legal advice and should be reviewed by an Australian legal professional before publication.

## 1. Purpose

This Privacy Policy describes how SBBC may collect, use, store, disclose, and protect personal information through the public website, student portal, parent / guardian portal, teacher workspace, enrollment flow, payment flow, live classes, recorded learning materials, and related services.

The final version should be adapted to the actual SBBC legal entity, systems, vendors, data storage locations, contact details, and Australian privacy obligations.

## 2. Personal Information SBBC May Collect

SBBC may collect information such as:

- Name
- Email address
- Phone number
- WeChat or other contact details
- Country, city, timezone, and preferred language
- Student age or date of birth where needed
- Parent or guardian contact details for underage students
- Emergency contact details where needed
- Course interests and learning goals
- Enrollment history
- Lesson package and remaining credit information
- Timetable, attendance, homework, feedback, and progress records
- Payment status, order records, invoices, receipts, and refund records
- Certificate information
- Consultation notes and trial lesson records
- Website form submissions and messages
- Account login and security information
- Device, browser, IP address, cookie, analytics, and usage information

SBBC should only collect information that is reasonably needed for its services and operations.

## 3. Information About Underage Students

For underage students, SBBC may collect student learning information and parent / guardian contact, confirmation, and payment information.

Underage students may start enrollment or select a course, but payment, terms acceptance, privacy policy acceptance, and minor-related confirmations should be completed by a parent or guardian.

SBBC should avoid collecting unnecessary information from underage students and should design access permissions carefully so that sensitive payment, billing, and consent information is handled by the parent or guardian.

## 4. How SBBC Collects Information

SBBC may collect information when users:

- Visit the website
- Submit an inquiry or trial lesson form
- Create an account
- Enroll in a course
- Make or receive a payment
- Attend a live or recorded class
- Communicate with teachers, teaching assistants, or admin staff
- Submit homework, feedback, or support requests
- Use the student portal, parent / guardian portal, or admin tools
- Interact with emails, messages, analytics, or security tools

SBBC may also receive information from parents, guardians, teachers, teaching assistants, payment providers, booking tools, video meeting tools, analytics tools, and other service providers.

## 5. How SBBC Uses Personal Information

SBBC may use personal information to:

- Respond to inquiries
- Arrange consultation and trial lessons
- Create and manage accounts
- Process enrollment
- Confirm parent / guardian responsibility for underage students
- Process payments, refunds, invoices, and receipts
- Deliver live and recorded courses
- Manage lesson credits, timetable, attendance, homework, and feedback
- Communicate class reminders, makeup lessons, renewals, and operational notices
- Support teachers, teaching assistants, and admin staff
- Issue certificates
- Manage points and learning records
- Improve website content, course design, and user experience
- Prevent misuse, fraud, spam, and unauthorized access
- Meet legal, tax, accounting, recordkeeping, and compliance obligations

## 6. Disclosure to Third Parties

SBBC may share personal information with service providers where needed to operate the platform, including:

- Payment providers
- Email and notification providers
- Website hosting and database providers
- Authentication and security providers
- Video meeting or recorded content providers
- Analytics and search performance tools
- Customer support or CRM tools
- Accounting, tax, legal, or compliance advisers
- Teachers, teaching assistants, and staff who need the information to deliver services

SBBC should not sell personal information.

Where required, SBBC should make sure third-party providers handle information appropriately.

## 7. International Storage and Cross-Border Disclosure

Some service providers may store or process information outside Australia.

The final version should list known important service providers and, where practical, the countries or regions where information may be processed.

Users should be informed that their information may be handled by overseas service providers if SBBC uses global cloud, payment, email, analytics, or video services.

## 8. Cookies, Analytics, and Security Tools

SBBC may use cookies and similar technologies to:

- Keep users logged in
- Remember preferences
- Improve website performance
- Understand website traffic and course interest
- Measure consultation and enrollment conversion
- Protect the site from spam, abuse, or automated attacks

Examples may include Google Search Console, Google Analytics, PostHog, Umami, reCAPTCHA, or similar tools, depending on the final technology choices.

If Google reCAPTCHA or similar tools are used, Google's privacy policy and terms may also apply.

## 9. Payment Information

SBBC should not store full card numbers unless it has a specific compliant reason and infrastructure to do so.

Payment details should normally be processed by a third-party payment provider such as Stripe or another selected provider.

SBBC may store payment status, order references, invoice records, receipts, refund records, and related accounting information.

## 10. Data Security

SBBC should take reasonable steps to protect personal information from misuse, interference, loss, unauthorized access, modification, or disclosure.

Security measures may include:

- Secure authentication
- Role-based access control
- Database permission rules
- Audit logs for sensitive actions
- Encrypted connections
- Staff access limits
- Backups
- Vendor security controls

No online service can guarantee absolute security, but SBBC should design the platform with privacy and security in mind from the beginning.

## 11. Access, Correction, and Deletion Requests

Users may request access to or correction of their personal information.

Parents or guardians may request access to information related to an underage student where appropriate.

Some information may need to be retained for legal, tax, accounting, dispute, safety, or operational reasons.

The final policy should explain how users can submit requests and how SBBC will respond.

## 12. Data Retention

SBBC should keep personal information only for as long as reasonably needed for the purpose it was collected, or as required for legal, tax, accounting, dispute, education, or operational reasons.

The final system should define retention rules for:

- Inquiry and consultation records
- Student profiles
- Parent / guardian records
- Payment and invoice records
- Attendance and lesson records
- Feedback and learning progress
- Certificates
- Audit logs
- Website analytics

## 13. Marketing Communications

SBBC may send course updates, event information, renewal reminders, or learning-related messages.

Users should be able to opt out of marketing messages where required, although SBBC may still send necessary service, payment, class, account, or safety messages.

## 14. Complaints

Users should be able to contact SBBC if they have concerns about privacy or data handling.

The final version should include a clear complaint contact email and response process.

If Australian privacy law applies, users may also have the right to contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).

## 15. Changes to This Policy

SBBC may update this Privacy Policy from time to time.

The latest version should be available on the SBBC website.

If changes materially affect users, SBBC should provide reasonable notice where practical.

## 16. Contact

The final published version should include:

- SBBC legal entity name
- Australian Business Number (ABN) or Australian Company Number (ACN), if applicable
- Business address or mailing address
- Privacy contact email
- Phone number
- Complaint handling process
