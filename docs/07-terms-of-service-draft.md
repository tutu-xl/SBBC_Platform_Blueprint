# SBBC Terms of Service Draft

> Draft for planning only. This document is not legal advice and should be reviewed by an Australian legal professional before publication.

## 1. Purpose

These Terms of Service describe the basic rules for using the SBBC website, student portal, parent / guardian portal, teacher workspace, enrollment flow, payment flow, live classes, recorded learning materials, and related services.

The final published version should be adapted to the actual legal entity, business registration details, contact details, refund policy, payment provider, course delivery model, and Australian legal requirements.

## 2. Who These Terms Apply To

These terms apply to:

- Visitors who browse the public SBBC website
- Students who create an account or enroll in a course
- Parents or guardians who register, confirm, or pay on behalf of an underage student
- Teachers, teaching assistants, and staff who use SBBC platform tools
- Any user who accesses SBBC content, courses, events, forms, or online services

## 3. Accounts and Accurate Information

Users must provide accurate and up-to-date information when registering, submitting an inquiry, booking a trial lesson, enrolling in a course, or making a payment.

Users are responsible for keeping login details secure and should not share their account with other people.

SBBC may contact users using the email address, phone number, WeChat account, or other contact details provided during registration or enrollment.

## 4. Adult and Underage Students

SBBC should distinguish between adult students and underage students.

Adult students may:

- Register their own account
- Enroll in courses
- Complete payment
- Manage their own orders, invoices, and receipts

Underage students may:

- Create or use a student learning account where appropriate
- Start enrollment or select a course
- View courses, timetable, lesson credits, feedback, learning materials, and points

Underage students may not complete course payment independently. Payment, terms acceptance, privacy policy acceptance, and any minor-related confirmations should be completed by a parent or guardian.

## 5. Parent / Guardian Responsibilities

For underage students, the parent or guardian is responsible for:

- Confirming the student's enrollment
- Completing payment for the student's course
- Accepting the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy
- Confirming any minor-related consent items
- Receiving payment, renewal, absence, and makeup-lesson notices
- Handling refunds, invoices, receipts, and billing matters
- Supervising the student's learning progress

## 6. Course Enrollment and Payment

Course details, prices, lesson credits, delivery mode, and availability should be shown clearly before payment where possible.

Payment may be processed through third-party payment providers such as Stripe or another provider selected by SBBC. Users may also be subject to the relevant payment provider's terms and privacy practices.

An enrollment is normally confirmed after SBBC receives payment or otherwise confirms the order.

SBBC should keep a record of orders, payments, invoices, receipts, and any adjustments.

## 7. Cancellation, Makeup Lessons, and Refunds

SBBC should publish a separate refund and cancellation policy, or include detailed rules in the final version of these terms.

The policy should cover:

- Trial lesson cancellation
- Regular lesson cancellation
- Absence and no-show rules
- Makeup lesson eligibility
- Course package expiry
- Refund request process
- Refund method and processing time

Nothing in SBBC's terms should exclude rights that cannot be excluded under Australian Consumer Law, including applicable consumer guarantees for services.

## 8. Live Classes and Third-Party Tools

SBBC may use third-party tools such as Zoom, Tencent Meeting, Google services, payment providers, email providers, hosting providers, or analytics tools.

When a user accesses a third-party service, that service may have its own terms and privacy policy.

SBBC should make reasonable efforts to provide class links, access instructions, and support information, but users are responsible for having a suitable device, internet connection, and login details.

## 9. Recorded Content and Learning Materials

SBBC may provide recorded lessons, worksheets, course materials, homework, feedback, certificates, and other learning resources.

Unless otherwise stated, these materials are provided for the enrolled student's personal learning use only.

Users should not copy, download, record, share, sell, repost, or distribute SBBC learning materials without permission.

## 10. User Conduct

Users must not:

- Misuse the website or platform
- Attempt to access another user's account
- Upload unlawful, harmful, misleading, or offensive content
- Interfere with classes, teachers, students, or platform operations
- Copy or redistribute SBBC content without permission
- Use SBBC services for spam, fraud, harassment, or illegal activity

SBBC may suspend or restrict access if a user breaches these terms or creates safety, legal, payment, or operational risk.

## 11. SBBC Store

SBBC may show merchandise on the main SBBC website and link to a separate `SBBC Store`.

The main SBBC platform should not directly handle merchandise checkout, inventory, shipping, or after-sales support unless this is specifically added later.

Purchases made on a separate `SBBC Store` website should be governed by that store's own terms, privacy policy, payment rules, and refund policy.

## 12. Intellectual Property

SBBC owns or licenses its website content, course materials, brand assets, videos, designs, text, graphics, and platform content.

Users keep ownership of personal information and user-submitted information where applicable, but grant SBBC permission to use submitted information as needed to provide services, manage enrollment, deliver classes, process payment, and operate the platform.

## 13. Privacy

SBBC's handling of personal information should be described in the Privacy Policy.

Users should read the Privacy Policy before registering, submitting an inquiry, enrolling in a course, or making a payment.

## 14. Changes to Services or Terms

SBBC may update course offerings, platform features, prices, policies, or these terms from time to time.

If changes materially affect users, SBBC should provide reasonable notice where practical.

The latest version should be available on the SBBC website.

## 15. Liability

SBBC should provide services with reasonable care and skill.

To the extent allowed by law, SBBC is not responsible for losses caused by user error, incorrect information provided by users, third-party service outages, device issues, internet connection problems, or events outside SBBC's reasonable control.

Nothing in these terms should limit rights that cannot be limited under Australian law.

The final published version should include:

- Business address or mailing address
- Contact email
- Phone number
- Support or complaint contact process
